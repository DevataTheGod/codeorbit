### Week 2: First Conversations & Mentor Validation

**Days 8–12: Take Mentor Calls (Target: 3–5 calls)**

**Call Structure (15–20 minutes):**

1. **Opening (2 min):**
   ```
   "Hi [Name], thanks for making time. 
    I'm building a system to help bootcamp mentors verify student understanding.
    I know you work at [bootcamp] as a [role], and I imagine you're dealing with 
    the AI-generated code problem. That's what I want to solve.
    
    Do you have 15 minutes?"
   ```

2. **Problem Discovery (5 min):**
   ```
   "Tell me about your biggest challenge with students right now."
   
   [Listen for: Understanding verification, mentor overload, AI issues, interview failures]
   
   "How do you currently know if a student truly understands their code?"
   
   [Listen for: "We don't really know", "We assume", "Mentors try their best"]
   ```

3. **Pain Validation (5 min):**
   ```
   "Have you had situations where a student's project looked great, 
    but they struggled in interviews?"
   
   [Listen intensity: How often? How many students?]
   
   "Has ChatGPT made this harder?"
   
   [If yes: That's a buying signal]
   ```

4. **Solution Intro (3 min):**
   ```
   "What if you could see an Understanding Score for each student—
    showing exactly which concepts they mastered and which they're shaky on?
    
    Our system:
    - Measures real understanding (not just code completion)
    - Gives mentors actionable insights (who to help first)
    - Detects AI-assisted work through explanations
    
    We're working with a few bootcamps to pilot this.
    Does this sound relevant?"
   ```

5. **Next Step (1 min):**
   ```
   If positive:
   "Would it be worth talking to [Founder/Academic Head] about this?
    Can you introduce me?"
   
   If neutral:
   "Understood. Can I follow up in a month with a demo?"
   
   If negative:
   "Thanks for your time. Good luck with your program."
   ```

**Call Outcome Tracking:**

After each call, record:
- ✅ Pain level (1–5): Do they feel the problem acutely?
- ✅ Bootcamp size: How many students? Mentors?
- ✅ AI problem: Mentioned? How much?
- ✅ Mentor overload: Mentioned? How serious?
- ✅ Warm intro: Will they introduce you? To whom?
- ✅ Next step: What did you agree?

**Days 13–14: Follow-up & Warm Intro Requests**

- [ ] Send thank-you notes to all calls (personalized)
- [ ] If they said "maybe introduce": Follow up with specific request
- [ ] "Would you be willing to introduce me to [name]? Here's what I'd say..."
- [ ] Provide intro template they can use (makes it easy)

## SECTION 4: FIRST CUSTOMER PLAYBOOK

### The Pilot is Your Product Research

Don't think of the pilot as a small deal. Think of it as:
- **Product research:** What works? What doesn't?
- **Sales proof:** Is there real demand?
- **Founder story:** "We helped [bootcamp] measure understanding at scale"
- **Data collection:** Do understanding scores predict job outcomes?

### Success Looks Like This

**Week 1–2 of Pilot:**
```
Bootcamp: "We loaded CodeOrbit, mentors are getting used to the dashboard"
You: "Great. What questions are they asking?"
Bootcamp: "How to interpret the understanding scores"
You: "I'll record a 5-min video explaining the scores. Send you tmr."

→ Quick iteration based on real feedback
```

**Week 3–4 of Pilot:**
```
Bootcamp: "Mentors are saying it saves time. Instead of reviewing 50 projects 
           one by one, they can see 'Sarah needs help with async, Raj needs 
           help with databases.'"
You: (Internally) → This is working. The hypothesis is validated.

→ Mentors are seeing value
```

**Week 5–6 of Pilot:**
```
Bootcamp: "Students are asking Orbit more questions. We think they're learning better."
You: "Let's measure this. Can you send me the interview data after hiring cycle?"

→ Relationship deepens; you're partners in success
```

**Week 7–8 of Pilot:**
```
Bootcamp: "We want to continue. Can we do this with our next 4 cohorts?"
You: "Absolutely. Let's convert to our standard pricing (₹99–149 per student)."

→ Pilot becomes a long-term customer
```

### How to Measure Pilot Success

**Quantitative:**
- [ ] Mentor time savings (tracked hours before vs. after)
- [ ] Understanding score accuracy (does it correlate with interview outcomes?)
- [ ] Student engagement (Orbit interactions per student per week)
- [ ] Platform uptime (99%+)
- [ ] Feature usage (% of dashboard features used)

**Qualitative:**
- [ ] Mentor feedback (quotes from mentors about value)
- [ ] Bootcamp founder confidence (would they recommend?)
- [ ] Student sentiment (do students find Orbit helpful?)
- [ ] Bootcamp interest in continuation (yes/no/maybe)

### Red Flags to Watch

If during pilot you see:
- ❌ Mentors not using the dashboard (adoption < 50%) → Product issue
- ❌ Understanding scores not tracking with interview outcomes → Algorithm issue
- ❌ Orbit responses mostly unhelpful → Prompt/model issue
- ❌ Bootcamp losing interest midway → Fit or value issue
- ❌ High support burden (daily issues) → Product stability issue

**If you see red flags:**
- Don't ignore them
- Talk to bootcamp: "What's not working?"
- Fix it quickly (or discount the pilot fee)
- Treat pilot as learning, not a loss
